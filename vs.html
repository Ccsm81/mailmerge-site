The honest take on each

MailMerge — best if you hate subscriptions & value privacy

Strengths: pay once (no recurring bill), works with any email provider (not just Gmail), 100% local so your contact list never touches a server, native Mac & Windows app, address validation + export. Where it’s weaker: no open/click tracking (intentionally — that requires a tracking server, which would break the “local” promise), and you send through your own account so deliverability is on you.

YAMM (Yet Another Mail Merge)

Best for: people fully inside Gmail + Google Sheets who want simple tracking. Trade-offs: Gmail-only, subscription, your data lives in Google’s cloud, and it needs broad access to your Google account.

GMass

Best for: heavier Gmail senders who want advanced features, automation and detailed analytics. Trade-offs: Gmail-only, monthly subscription that adds up fast, browser-based.

Mailmeteor

Best for: a clean, simple Gmail mail-merge with tracking. Trade-offs: Gmail-only, subscription, cloud-based.

So which should you pick?

If you live entirely in Gmail and you want open/click tracking, YAMM, GMass or Mailmeteor are solid — they’re good products. But if you:

• send from Outlook, a custom domain, or anything that isn’t Gmail, or
• are tired of paying every month to email from your own account, or
• handle sensitive contact lists (clients, candidates, invoices) that shouldn’t sit on someone else’s server —

…then a desktop, pay-once, fully-local tool is the better fit. That’s exactly what MailMerge is built for.
